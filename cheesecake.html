Blueberry Cheesecake

A perfect balance between the slight sweetness of the blueberries and the complex flavors of the cream cheese and the graham shell

A staple meal of those who have birthdays, most especially in rainy seasons.

Only the most generic cream cheese brands, graham cracker brands, and a nonentity canned blueberry brand, and should be served cold.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • ¼ cup melted butter
  • 2 tbsp white sugar
  • 2 (8 oz) packaged cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • ¾ cup white sugar
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 4 white eggs
  • 2 cups frozen blueberries, dry pack
  • ⅓ cup blueberry jelly

Recipe instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 325 degree F (165 degrees C).
  2. Mix graham crumbs, melted butter, and 2 tbsp white sugar in a bowl until well combined. Pat mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan.
  3. Mash cream cheese in a mixing bowl until soft and creamy. Gradually beat in sour cream, 3/4 cup sugar, flour, and vanilla. Beat in eggs, one at a time. Pour mixture into the crumb-lined pan.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven until firm to the touch, about 1 hour.
  5. Place baked cheesecake in the refrigerator until thoroughly chilled, about 4 hours.
  6. Remove cheesecake from the pan by loosening the edges with a knife and opening the springform latch. Transfer to a serving platter. Place frozen blueberries on top. Melt jelly in a saucepan over low heat; spoon over blueberries to glaze. Chill in the refrigerator until ready to serve.
